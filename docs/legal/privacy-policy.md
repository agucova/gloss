# Gloss — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-04-17
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17

Gloss ("we", "us", "our") operates the Gloss browser extension and the web
application at **https://gloss.space** (collectively, the "Service"). This
policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you
have.

If you have questions, contact us at **gloss@agucova.dev**.

---

## 1. Summary

- Gloss lets you highlight text on webpages, save bookmarks, and optionally
  share those highlights with friends you add inside the Service.
- We collect only what the Service needs to function: your account details, the
  highlights and bookmarks you create, and the URLs of pages you choose to
  highlight or bookmark.
- We do **not** sell your personal data, show you ads, or use your data to
  train machine-learning models.
- We do **not** track your general browsing history. The extension only records
  the URL and context of pages where **you explicitly create a highlight,
  bookmark, or comment**.

## 3. Information we collect

We collect the following categories of information. We do **not** collect
information beyond what is listed here.

### 3.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect:

- **Name**, **email address**, and (if you provide them) **profile image**,
  **username**, **bio**, **website**, and **social handles** (e.g., X/Twitter,
  GitHub).
- **Authentication credentials** handled by our auth provider
  ([Better-Auth](https://www.better-auth.com/)), including a hashed password
  (if you sign in with email + password), passkeys you register, and OAuth
  identifiers if you sign in with Google or Apple.
- **Email verification status** and related verification tokens.

### 3.2 Content you create

- **Highlights**: the URL of the page, the highlighted text, position data
  (W3C Web Annotation selectors — range, text-position, text-quote — used to
  re-locate the highlight when you revisit the page), and your chosen
  visibility (`private`, `friends`, or `public`).
- **Bookmarks**: the URL, title, description, favicon, Open Graph image, and
  site name of pages you save.
- **Comments**: markdown text you write on highlights, including any
  @-mentions of other users.
- **Tags**: names and colors of tags you create to organize bookmarks.

### 3.3 Social graph

- **Friendships**: the user IDs of people you send, accept, or reject friend
  requests to/from, and the status of those requests.

### 3.4 Preferences

- Display and privacy settings: theme preference (light/dark/system),
  highlight display filter, comment display mode, and default visibility
  settings for your profile, highlights, and bookmarks.

### 3.5 Programmatic access

- **API keys** you create for the Gloss CLI / MCP server. We store a
  **cryptographic hash** of each key (not the key itself), along with its
  name, prefix, scope (`read` or `write`), and last-used timestamp.

### 3.6 Optional integrations

- **Curius import**: if you choose to import your highlights from Curius, we
  temporarily store a Curius API token you provide so we can fetch your data.
  You can delete this credential from your account at any time.

### 3.7 Automatically collected

- **Timestamps** associated with each record (creation, update,
  last-used).
- **Technical logs** generated by our hosting providers (see §6), including
  IP addresses and request metadata, for security, debugging, and abuse
  prevention. These logs are not combined with your content.

### 3.8 What we do **not** collect

- We do **not** record pages you visit unless you highlight, bookmark, or
  comment on them.
- We do **not** collect keystrokes, mouse movements, form contents, or page
  contents beyond the specific text you highlight.
- We do **not** use analytics, advertising, or cross-site tracking SDKs.
- We do **not** collect precise location data.

## 4. Browser extension permissions

The Gloss extension requests the following Chrome permissions. We use each one
only for the purpose described:

| Permission                     | Why we need it                                                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `<all_urls>` (host permission) | Required to inject the content script that lets you highlight text on any webpage you visit. We only **read** and **store** content you explicitly highlight. |
| `storage`                      | Store local settings (theme, session state) in the browser.                                                                                                   |
| `activeTab`                    | Interact with the page you're currently viewing when you invoke Gloss.                                                                                        |
| `cookies`                      | Maintain your signed-in session with the Gloss backend.                                                                                                       |
| `offscreen`                    | Run isolated tasks (e.g., parsing selectors) without disrupting the page.                                                                                     |

The extension does **not** read the full content of pages you visit, modify
pages without your action, or send any information to our servers unless you
explicitly create a highlight, bookmark, or comment, or are signed in and
viewing a page where friends' shared highlights should be overlaid.

## 5. How we use your information

We use the information above to:

- Provide core Service functionality: create, store, display, search, and
  synchronize your highlights, bookmarks, comments, and tags across your
  devices.
- Render friends' highlights on pages you visit, when they have shared them
  with you.
- Authenticate you and secure your account.
- Communicate with you about your account (e.g., email verification,
  magic-link sign-in, security notices).
- Prevent abuse, debug issues, and enforce our Terms of Service.
- Comply with legal obligations.

We do **not** use your content to train machine-learning or AI models, and we
do **not** use it for advertising.

## 7. Visibility controls

Every highlight and bookmark has an explicit visibility setting that you
control:

- **Private** — visible only to you.
- **Friends** — visible to users whose friend requests you have accepted.
- **Public** — visible to anyone with a Gloss account, and potentially to
  unauthenticated visitors on public profile pages.

You can change a highlight's or bookmark's visibility at any time. Changing
visibility to "private" removes it from friends' and public views going
forward, but cached copies in other users' browsers may persist briefly until
their next sync.

## 8. Data retention

- We keep your account and content for as long as your account is active.
- If you delete a highlight, bookmark, or comment, it is removed from the
  active database. Soft-deleted comments are retained for up to **30 days**
  to allow recovery, then hard-deleted.
- If you delete your account, we delete your profile, highlights, bookmarks,
  comments, tags, API keys, and Curius credentials within **30 days**.
  Content you posted in shared contexts (e.g., replies to another user's
  highlight) may be anonymized rather than deleted, so the surrounding
  conversation remains intelligible.
- Backups and operational logs may retain residual data for up to **90 days**
  before being overwritten.

## 9. Security

- All traffic between your browser and our servers is encrypted in transit
  with TLS.
- API keys are stored as cryptographic hashes; we cannot recover a lost key.
- Passwords are hashed by our authentication provider using industry-standard
  algorithms.
- Access to production systems is limited to the people who need it.

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a security breach that
affects your personal data, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

If you are in the EEA or UK, our international transfers to US-based
